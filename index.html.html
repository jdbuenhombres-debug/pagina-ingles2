📘 UNIT 1: First Conditional - Possible Future Situations

Activity 1: First Conditional Quiz and Grammar Study

In this activity, I studied the use of the First Conditional. This grammatical structure is used to talk about possible situations in the present or future and their possible results.

The basic structure is: If + Present Simple, will + base verb.

The activity helped me identify future clauses, the use of "will", and how conditions can express realistic possibilities.

Activity 2: Rubén's Plans - Forum Participation

The purpose of this activity was to write two paragraphs about Rubén's plans using at least five First Conditional sentences.

Hello. Tomorrow, if the weather is not cold, Rubén will go to the park with his friends. If he finishes his homework early, he will play soccer in the afternoon. If his cousin comes to visit, they can ride their bikes together. Rubén might buy ice cream if he has enough money. If the sun shines all day, they will stay outside until the evening.

This weekend, if Rubén wakes up early, he will go hiking with his family. If it does not rain, they may have a picnic near the lake. If Rubén feels tired, he should rest for a while before playing games. His mother will cook hamburgers if everyone gets hungry.

Examples: If it rains, I will take my umbrella. If I see Mauricio, I will tell him about it.

📗 UNIT 2: Second Conditional - Dreams and Imaginary Situations

Activity 1: Second Conditional Quiz and Grammar Study

In this activity, I learned that the Second Conditional is used for imaginary, unlikely or theoretical situations.

Structure: If + Past Simple, would + infinitive.

Example: If I were rich, I would travel around the world.

Activity 2: Rubén's Pool Day - Forum

Rubén and Melissa spent a wonderful afternoon at the swimming pool. They played games, took pictures and enjoyed the sunset.

They imagined future activities using the Second Conditional.

If Rubén had more free weekends, he would organize a beach trip with Melissa.

If Melissa lived closer to the coast, they could visit different beaches every month.

If they had enough money, they would travel to a tropical island.

If Melissa knew how to surf, she might teach Rubén at the beach.

📕 UNIT 3: Third Conditional - Past Experiences

Activity 1: Third Conditional Quiz and Grammar Study

I studied the Third Conditional, which is used to talk about past situations that did not happen and imagine different results.

Structure: If + Past Perfect, would have + Past Participle.

Activity 2: Rubén's Visit to the Forbidden City

Rubén has finished his visit to the Forbidden City. He has learned about Chinese culture and history.

Before returning to the hotel, he had completed his tour and had reflected on everything he had seen.

If Rubén had not met Chun Tao, he would not have understood the history so well.

If she had not given him books, he would not have learned so much.

If he had arrived later, he would have missed important parts of the visit.

If Melissa had gone with him, she would have enjoyed the architecture.

If he had known more before the trip, he might have planned a longer visit.
